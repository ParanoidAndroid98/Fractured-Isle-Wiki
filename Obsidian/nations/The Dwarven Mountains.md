# The Dwarven Mountains

#nations #dwarves

![[assets/dwarven-mountains-intro.jpg]]

The dwarven island is the one place in the Fractured Isles where the policy is, officially, that **you have not been invited**. The dwarves are not hostile in their exclusion — the clockwork emissaries they send to manage external trade are courteous and efficient. The island simply is not open to visitors, and has not been since the retreat following the Fracturing.

---

## Overview

What is known of the interior comes from the accounts of the dwarves who have, in recent centuries, begun to travel. They describe:
- Grand halls of diamond and worked gold
- Feast tables that seat three thousand
- Forge complexes running continuously for two thousand years
- A community that is, by their own reckoning, only recently ready to re-engage with the world

---

## Government

![[assets/dwarf-yussin-opalbane.jpg]]

**Yussin Opalbane** serves as the external face of dwarven governance, which is not the same as its internal structure. The actual decision-making within the mountain is conducted by the **Council of Seams** — a body whose precise composition and function is not shared with non-dwarves, and whose decisions reach the outside world through Opalbane.

**Yussin Opalbane:** Ornate armour with embedded gemstones that are part of their body rather than jewellery. Greyish-brown skin. An expression of careful diplomatic courtesy that is also, to those who look carefully, the expression of someone carrying a great deal of internal information they are not sharing.

---

## Dwarves in the Wider World

The emergence of individual dwarves into the wider world has been the most significant cultural development of the past few centuries. Dwarves who travel tend to be young by dwarven standards — in their second or third century — and arrive with an **intensity of curiosity** that sometimes catches their hosts off guard. They are not naive. They have been raised on the Isles' history. They are choosing to see it for themselves.
