# The Elven Isle

#nations #elves #elven-isle

![[elven-isle-rulers.jpg|assets/elven-isle-intro.jpg]]

The elven isle is a **supervised territory**. This is not hidden. The giants whose nomadic lands surround the island maintain a permanent watch over its coastlines, by agreement with the New Gods and with the other dragon provinces, that has been continuous since the Fracturing.

---

## Overview

The island is split into two halves with no geographical feature marking the division — the split is magical, an artefact of the curse that settled on elven civilisation after the Fracturing:

- **One half:** Perpetual warm dawn light *(Aelwrin Glade)*
- **Other half:** Perpetual dusk *(Celesphain)*

The flora and fauna on each half have adapted over three thousand years, producing two ecosystems unlike anywhere else in the Isles. The elves who live on each half have adapted alongside them.

At the exact centre of the island, the **[[organisations/The Ebony Order|Ebony Order]] maintains a spire** that predates any other structure currently standing — monitoring the curse's progress and ensuring it never becomes something other than what it is. The elves consider the spire's presence complicated.

---

## The Elven Curse

When the elven empire's arcane engine failed and tore the material plane apart, the gods passed judgement. The curse laid on the elven people — that their **bodies would be consumed by metal** — was not simple punishment. It was reflection. The gold, silver, or bronze that spreads through elven flesh is the colour of the empire they built.

| Lineage | Metal | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Common elven line | Gold | Slow spread; can be slowed further by how one lives |
| House of Celzala (dark elves) | Silver | Shaped by the gods into something workable and beautiful |
| House of Varelis (wood elves) | Bronze | Can be shaped by the bearer; enables protective carapace in the oldest |

The oldest elves — those who spent their longest years in genuine service to others — have learned to **shape the metal rather than be consumed by it**. The oldest wood elves are more metal than flesh. They are not monsters. They are gardeners.

---

## Celesphain (Dusk City)

![[elven-dusk-city.png]] 

**Ruler:** **Nuovis Vatris** — a dark elf with moth-like features, furry collar, and the quiet authority of someone who has been tending the dying for three thousand years.

The dark elves of Celesphain carry their silver metal openly and shape it into masks, jewellery, and architectural features. The city is, by any aesthetic standard, extraordinary.

---

## Aelwrin Glade *(Dawn City)*

![[elven-dawn-city.png]]

**Ruler:** **Laure Gwath** — a dawn elf with blue jay features, a cape of real feathers, and talon-like nails. Known for a diplomatic skill that has kept the island's external relations functional despite everything.

The dawn elves of Aelwrin Glade have integrated their bronze metal into a tradition of **living architecture** — the metal growing with the trees, the buildings shaped from the combination of wood and worked bronze that their bodies produce.

---

## The Ethereal Marsh

Between the two cities lies the [[locations/The Ethereal Marsh|Ethereal Marsh]] — a strip of marshland existing in a permanent state of between, where the light is always either dawn or dusk, shifting at irregular intervals. The marsh is said to cleanse the soul of those who pass through it honestly, stripping away comfortable stories about one's own motivations and leaving only what is actually there.

The reflections in the water do not always show what is above them.
