## The Primordial Crucible — Can It Reform?

**The short answer:** Yes. This is what everything is building toward.

The Primordial Crucible was shattered into four elemental fragments. The fragments were imprisoned in four separate elemental planes. The theory was that separated and imprisoned, they could never reconstitute.

The theory was wrong, or rather, it was right about the timeframe but not about the possibility. Given sufficient time — and the Outer Gods have had ten thousand years — the fragments *remember* each other. The resonance signals that began appearing approximately 3000 years ago are the fragments communicating across their planar barriers.

**What this means:** The Crucible is slowly, over geological time, attempting to reform. Not quickly. Not in a single campaign arc. But the trajectory is real, and three parties are aware of it:

1. **The Ebony Order** — has known since approximately 2800 AF, has classified the evidence, has not decided what to do about it.
2. **The Chromatic Inquisition's senior leadership** — their hidden mandate exists precisely because of this. They have been monitoring it for three thousand years.
3. **The Marred** — the Crucible's last act of creation. They are drawn toward the Vault sites not by intelligence but by resonance — they are *pieces of the thing that is trying to reform*, and they feel the pull.

**The critical question the players may eventually face:** If the Crucible reforms, what happens? The Eioran feared it and destroyed it. Were they right to? The Crucible's last acts were creating the Marred (ambiguous) and trusting the Eioran (catastrophically misplaced, but arguably generous). An entity capable of that level of trust and that level of creation is not obviously a threat. It is also not obviously safe.

## The Eioran Vaults — What Was Really Built There

The Vaults are not workshops. They never were.

The Eioran built the Vaults as **containment facilities for the tools used to imprison the Outer Gods**. The arcane technology required to imprison beings of that magnitude was too dangerous to use and too significant to destroy. The Vaults were designed to keep those tools out of reach of anyone who might misuse them — including, explicitly, the Eioran themselves.

When the Eioran dissolved into the New Gods, the Vaults were sealed with protections keyed to Eioran biology. No non-Eioran entity could open them. This worked perfectly for ten thousand years.

**The pattern that has emerged since 2200 AF:** The Vaults are opening themselves. Not being opened — *opening*. The tools inside are becoming accessible. The Pathfinders have mapped the emergence pattern. The Ebony Order has mapped what it points toward.

It points toward the sites of the four elemental plane boundaries — the locations where the Crucible's fragments are closest to the material plane's surface.

**What this means for the campaign:** The tools that imprisoned the Outer Gods once are becoming available again. Whether this is because the Crucible is trying to be re-imprisoned by its own design, or because something else is pulling them out, or because the Vaults themselves have reached some kind of time limit — no one knows. Yet.

The party that recovers Vault artefacts before the Order gets there is holding pieces of the most significant power in the history of the world. The Order wants them. The Inquisition wants them. At least one Marred appears to be drawn to them.

## Ysolde's Final Painting — *The End of All*

The painting is in the Brass Dragon Grand Library, under the highest classification level, in a room with seventeen redundant arcane locks. Ximoal has the only key that can open all seventeen simultaneously.

The painting shows five figures standing at the site of the largest Vault emergence. Behind them, four elemental tears in the sky — the planes becoming visible through breaches in the material world. The figures are not named. Three of them match living people.

**The Order's confirmed identifications (classified at Grand Archivist level):**

- **Figure 1** — matches the physical description of a specific Inquisitor currently serving in the Blue Dragon Province. The match is exact, including a scar on the left hand that the Inquisitor received four years ago, decades after Ysolde disappeared.
- **Figure 3** — matches a Pathfinder named in the current membership rolls whose route has, in the past two years, taken them to seven of the last nine Vault emergence sites.
- **Figure 5** — Ximoal has refused to identify this figure to anyone, including the Inquisitor General.

**Figures 2 and 4** have not been matched. The Order believes they haven't been born yet, or haven't been identified because they haven't yet done whatever will make them identifiable.

**The title:** Ysolde titled every painting. This one is called *The End of All*. Ysolde also titled a painting of a city that burned *The City's Last Day*, and a painting of a flood *The Waters Rise*. The titles are descriptive, not metaphorical.

**What happened to Ysolde:** Moran took them. Not as punishment — as protection. Ysolde saw something in the visions that the Inquisition would have felt compelled to prevent, and the prevention would have required silencing Ysolde in ways that Moran found unacceptable. They are in the Greylands. They are waiting. For what, Moran has not said.

## The Inquisition's True Mandate

The Chromatic Inquisition was founded ostensibly to manage arcane law and religious disputes. Its actual founding mandate, held by the senior leadership in an unbroken chain since its establishment, is this:

**Monitor the Marred. Ensure they cannot be used as instruments to reform the Primordial Crucible.**

The fear is specific: the four Marred are fragments of the Crucible. If someone with sufficient knowledge and sufficient power were to bring all four together at a site of sufficient planar resonance, they could potentially serve as a catalyst for the Crucible's reconstitution — either deliberately or by accident.

The Inquisition has been preventing this, quietly, for three thousand years. Most of what looks like Marred cult suppression is actually **Marred movement suppression** — the Inquisition doesn't primarily care about the cultists, they care about not allowing the Marred to converge.

**The complication at 3000 AF:** The Marred have been moving with increasing coherence since 2500 AF. The Inquisition's standard tools for disrupting their movement are becoming less effective. The entities are responding to something the Inquisition cannot block — the Crucible resonance — rather than to the cultists or to any individual actor's plans.

**What the Inquisitor General knows that no one else does:** The senior Vault artefact recovered in 2850 AF contains what appears to be an Eioran record of the original imprisonment. If correctly translated — and they are close — it will explain whether the Crucible's reformation would be catastrophic, beneficial, or something that defies that binary entirely.

The translation is being done by one person, in isolation, under conditions of extreme classification. That person has not filed a progress report in six weeks.

## The Outer God Prisons — What's Actually Happening

The prisons were designed by the Eioran to be permanent. They are not failing because the Outer Gods have found a weakness. They are failing because the **material plane itself is changing** in response to the Crucible resonance.

The prisons are anchored to the material plane's stability. As the plane shifts in response to the fragments' communication, the anchors shift with it. Not catastrophically. Not quickly. But measurably, and accelerating.

**Current status:**
- The Prime Purpose's prison: stable. Law-adjacent structure means it's actually reinforcing as the world's institutions become more rigid.
- The Manic Blaze: degrading fastest. Creative chaos responds most directly to the resonance.
- The Formless Mother: fluctuating. Tied to life-force, which is abundant. The Fey Realm is becoming more permeable, not less contained.
- The Eternal Patience: unchanged. Moran's influence keeps the Greylands stable.

**The Ebony Order does not know how to fix this.** What they know is that the tools to reimprison the Outer Gods may be in the Vaults, which are now opening. The question is whether any party capable of using those tools can be trusted with them — a question the Eioran answered badly once, and which the Order is not confident can be answered better this time.

## Adventure Framework: The Five Figures

The cleanest campaign arc the setting supports is built around Ysolde's painting:

1. **The party encounters a Vault** — standard Tier 1 hook.
2. **The Order arrives and wants the artefact** — the party may or may not comply, but they learn that the Order is paying unusual attention.
3. **The party learns about the painting** — through an Inquisitor contact, a Brass Library connection, or direct investigation into why the Order is so interested in Vault sites.
4. **The party realises someone in their orbit matches one of the figures** — or that one of them does.
5. **The stakes become clear** — what the painting shows, what it means, and what the choice at its centre actually is.

The choice at the centre: the Crucible is reforming. The tools to stop it — or guide it, or survive it — are in the Vaults. The party will have to decide, with incomplete information, whether to use those tools to prevent the reformation, assist it, or stand aside and let whatever happens happen.

There is no clean answer. This was true for the Eioran. It will be true for the party.
