# The Modern Age — 1800 AF to Present (3000 AF)

#history #modern-age

> *Previous: [[Age of Recovery]] · Next: (present day)*

The present day — **3000 AF** — is not peaceful, but it is the most stable the Fractured Isles have been since before the Fracturing. The dragon provinces function as an international community with enough shared infrastructure that war between them is expensive in ways that provide genuine deterrence. The [[organisations/Pathfinders of Aestri|Pathfinders of Aestri]], founded at this age's opening, have mapped most of the known world.

Underneath this stability, things are beginning to move.

---

## The Foundation of the Pathfinders — 1800 AF

Founded in explicit homage to [[faith/Saints#Saint Aestri|Saint Aestri]], the Pathfinders took on the work Aestri had done alone and institutionalised it — creating a permanent organisation for **exploration, cartography, and the documentation of the world's ongoing changes**.

Over twelve hundred years, the Pathfinders have:
- Produced the most comprehensive maps of the known world
- Built the most complete record of [[locations/Landmark Locations#The Eioran Vaults|Vault]] appearances and disappearances
- Trained generations of explorers whose accounts form the most reliable source of information about remote territories
- Maintained political neutrality consistently enough that even the [[organisations/The Chromatic Inquisition|Chromatic Inquisition]] generally trusts their geographic records

## Famous Figures of the Modern Age

### Elyrian Voss — c.2000 AF
**The First Return.** Many people had entered [[locations/Landmark Locations#The Eioran Vaults|Eioran Vaults]] before Voss. None had come back out — the assumption had settled into received wisdom that Vaults were one-way. Voss returned with documentation of an interior unlike anything the Pathfinders' external surveys had suggested was possible, and an account that prompted two hundred years of debate about what the Vaults actually were.

Their account remains the primary source for Vault interior research, which is to say: the field is built on a single survivor's testimony from a thousand years ago.

### Gariss Thorn — c.2600–2750 AF
The [[organisations/The Ebony Order|Ebony Order's]] most celebrated field agent and its most discussed cautionary example — sometimes in the same sentence. Known for "the Purge and the Honourable End" — a case that involved the suppression of a Marred cult that had embedded itself in a provincial government, and the methods Thorn used to accomplish it.

The Purge was effective. The methods used are the basis of three different Inquisition ethics reviews and at least one ongoing internal inquiry. Thorn is genuinely celebrated for what was achieved and genuinely studied for what was done to achieve it — a combination that keeps their file perpetually open.

### Solenne Asp — c.2400–2500 AF
**The Lost Mage.** A halfling who became obsessed with the asteroid belt — the debris left by the Fracturing that rings the planet — and eventually ascended to it via experimental magic, spending an indeterminate period among the debris before returning. Returned changed. Refused to say what they had seen, producing instead a series of paintings that the Brass Library has classified.

What is known: Asp's arcane development after the ascent was **qualitatively different** from before it, suggesting exposure to conditions not present in the material plane. What those conditions were, and what the paintings show, are not public knowledge.

## The Unsettling Pattern — 2200 AF Onward

The [[organisations/The Ebony Order|Ebony Order]] has been tracking something it has not yet made public. The sequence:

### 2200 AF — Vault Anomalies Begin
The [[locations/Landmark Locations#The Eioran Vaults|Eioran Vaults]] begin appearing with **increasing frequency** and in locations that do not match any previous pattern. The Pathfinders notice first — their Vault tracking goes back to the organisation's founding, and the frequency increase is statistically significant. They report it to the Order. The Order acknowledges receipt.

### 2500 AF — Marred Cult Resurgence
[[cosmology/The Marred|Marred]] cults across all four varieties see a resurgence that the Order's own modelling cannot fully explain. The standard drivers — economic disruption, political instability, individual grief — are present but not at levels that would account for the scale of increase. Something else is drawing people toward these communities.

### 2750 AF — Greylands Disturbances
The [[cosmology/The New Gods#Moran|Greylands]] — the realm of the dead — begin sending signals that the Order's specialists describe as "disturbances." The more candid among them call them **warnings**. The nature of what Moran is warning about is not disclosed. The Lich Queen of the [[nations/The Undead Lands|Undead Lands]] begins receiving communications she cannot trace to any known Greylands contact.

### 2900 AF — Prison Degradation
The arcane prisons that hold the [[cosmology/The Outer Gods|Outer Gods]] — structures that were designed to be permanent, that have stood for ten thousand years — begin showing signs of **structural weakening that should not be possible**. The Order's most senior specialists have no mechanism to explain it. They have hypotheses.

### 2990 AF — The Prophecies Return
Major prophecies begin emerging across multiple traditions, many traceable back to the tradition of [[faith/Saints#Saint Ysolde|Saint Ysolde's]] followers. They speak of **five figures, an elemental convergence, and a choice that cannot be undone**. Ysolde's final painting, *The End of All*, shows five lone figures facing something that consumes the horizon.

> *"The Order has verified that at least three of the figures in the painting match descriptions of people currently alive."*

## The World at 3000 AF

The Fractured Isles, right now, are:

**Visibly stable.** The provincial system functions. Trade flows. The [[organisations/The Ebony Order|Ebony Order]] and [[organisations/The Arcane Senate|Arcane Senate]] regulate magic. The [[organisations/The Chromatic Inquisition|Chromatic Inquisition]] investigates abuses. The [[organisations/The Banks|banks]] provide financial infrastructure. War between provinces is expensive enough to be deterred.

**Quietly accelerating.** Every indicator the Order tracks has been moving in the same direction for eight hundred years, and the rate of movement is increasing. The people who understand what the indicators mean have not agreed on what to do about them. Some have not agreed on whether anything can be done.

**Beautiful.** The Purified Garden produces new life in visible time. The Brass Library contains the most complete record of the world's history. The festivals are celebrated. Art is made. The hot springs of the Gold Province are open. The forest of the Green Province is tended. The dark elves guide the dying across every province with grace.

> *"It is 3000 AF, and the Fractured Isles are beautiful, complicated, and very quietly beginning to run out of time."*

## What the Players Enter

This is the world. The history is knowable — the Brass Library has most of it. The stakes are real but not yet widely understood. The five figures in Ysolde's painting are alive, somewhere, doing ordinary things that will eventually bring them to a convergence point that the painter saw eight hundred years ago.

The institutions built over ten thousand years of catastrophe and recovery will either be sufficient for what comes next, or they will not. The Eioran made their choice in their moment. The choice that is coming will be made by whoever is standing there when it arrives.
