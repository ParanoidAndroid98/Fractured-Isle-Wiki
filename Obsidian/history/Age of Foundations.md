# Age of Foundations — 0 to 1500 AB

#history #age-of-foundations

> *Previous: [[History Overview]] · Next: [[Age of Expansion]]*

The immediate aftermath of the [[cosmology/The Outer Gods#The Great Betrayal|Great Betrayal]]. The world was nearly empty. The war between the Eioran and the gods had reduced the material plane to a devastated remnant of what the [[cosmology/The Outer Gods#The Primordial Crucible|Primordial Crucible]] had built. The surviving Eioran collective made their choice — dissolution into the fabric of the world — and what emerged from that sacrifice were the [[cosmology/The New Gods|New Gods]] and, from the flesh and memory of the Eioran themselves, the proto-races.

---

## The Proto-Races Emerge

### The Dragons
The dragons inherited the Eioran's understanding of magic — not instinctively but as a deep structural knowledge, a capacity to perceive the forces that held the world together and, carefully, to work with them. They spent the early centuries not building empires but **repairing the plane**, using that knowledge to close the wounds the great war had left.

### The Giants
The giants inherited the Eioran's physical scale and the great strength that had been used to move continents during the war. They turned that strength to reconstruction in the most literal sense — reshaping the geography of the world, filling the great voids, closing the deep scars. The walkways they cut across the ancient rock, the paths that later became the first roads of the mortal world, date from this period.

### The Elves
The elves inherited the Eioran's organisational genius — the capacity to see complex systems and hold them in mind simultaneously. They became the first farmers, the first administrators, the first to begin cataloguing what remained and planning what came next. The seeds they planted in the earliest centuries of this age grew into the forests that still stand today.

## The Younger Races Arrive

The other races came later, and more quietly:

| Race | Origin |
|------|--------|
| **Dwarves** | Emerged from the deep rock, following the mineral seams the Crucible had laid down when it built the world |
| **Gnomes** | Appeared near the old forges and blast sites of the war, drawn to the residual heat and the scattered tools |
| **Tieflings & Changelings** | Arose near the old planar scars, carrying something of the [[cosmology/The Outer Gods#The Formless Mother|Formless Mother's]] influence in their blood |
| **Orcs** | Came from the places where order and chaos had met most violently |
| **Humans & Halflings** | Appeared in the dense, recovering forests |
| **Aarakocra** | Evolved from the wildlife of the high air |
| **Genasi** | Arose from the hotspots where elemental power remained concentrated near the Crucible's old prison seams |

## First Encounters with the Marred

The [[cosmology/The Marred|Marred]] appeared during this age as well, and were not immediately understood for what they were. They were powerful, alien, and did not communicate in ways the new races could decode. The early encounters were not wars — the races were too new and too scattered for war — but they were **shaping**.

The communities that survived proximity to the Marred survived by developing particular resiliences and particular pathologies. Those inheritances are still legible in the cultures that exist today.

## Legacy

The foundational inheritance of each proto-race — draconic arcane knowledge, giant physical endurance, elven systems-thinking — did not disappear as the races diversified. It became part of the cultural substrate. A dragon's instinctive grasp of magical structure is the same gift passed down ten thousand years. A dwarven community's insistence on deep foundations is the same giant-inherited understanding that the surface of things is less reliable than what lies beneath.

The Marred inheritances are less comfortable to acknowledge, but they are equally persistent.
