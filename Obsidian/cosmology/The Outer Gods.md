# The Outer Gods

#cosmology #outer-gods

Before the universe had shape, the Outer Gods existed — less creatures than **fundamental principles given will and hunger**. Their endless war against one another in the formless dark before creation is the reason the universe took the shape it did. Each is now imprisoned within a plane that mirrors their nature. Their cults still find them.

---

## The Prime Purpose

![[assets/outer-god-the-prime-purpose.jpg]]
*"The Architect · The Unyielding Pattern · The First Law"*

The embodiment of law in its most absolute form — not peace, but **the elimination of all deviation**. They conceived of life as chaos to be resolved, either through submission to a unified pattern or through erasure.

**Plane:** The Plane of Law — a realm of perfect, suffocating order where nothing changes and nothing can.  
**Influence:** Obsessive hierarchy, cultural uniformity, fear of difference  
**Note:** Civilisations under their sway have produced marvels of organisation and horrifying records of what was done to those who did not fit.

*Counterpart among the New Gods: [[The New Gods#Shangotha|Shangotha]]*

## The Manic Blaze

![[assets/outer-god-the-manic-blaze.jpg]]
*"The Burning Wheel · The Ever-Maker · The Consuming Spark"*

Chaos distilled — not destruction for its own sake, but the **compulsive cycle of creation and annihilation** that cannot distinguish between the two. The reason technology sometimes erupts forward in a generation and then burns down everything it built.

**Plane:** The Plane of Chaos — constant violent transformation where nothing survives long enough to be understood.  
**Influence:** Technological obsession, creative genius, cycles of creation and collapse  
**Note:** Those touched by the Blaze feel vital and alive with possibility. The destruction comes later.

*Counterpart among the New Gods: [[The New Gods#Neithona|Neithona]]*

---

## The Formless Mother

![[assets/outer-god-the-formless-mother.jpg]]
*"The First Mother · The Overflowing Cup · The Dreaming Wild"*

Life itself in all its excess and contradiction — **creation without restraint**. Her earliest gifts were the first beings and their capacity to dream, to desire, to become. Her later gifts were mutations and unbridled changes; not malice, but enthusiasm.

**Plane:** The Fey Realm / Plane of Life — time warps, reality bends, everything is in constant beautiful violent flux.  
**Influence:** Wild magic, mutation, the drive to become, creative excess  
**Note:** Her influence bleeds through into wild magic and the tiefling and changeling bloodlines.

*Counterpart among the New Gods: [[The New Gods#Vitasaol|Vitasaol]]*

---

## The Eternal Patience

![[assets/outer-god-the-eternal-patience.jpg]]
*"The Last Shore · The Patient One · The Gift-Giver"*

The idea of death — not as ending, but as **completion**. The one who will, eventually, turn off every light. Holds no malice toward existence. Was the first to grant living beings the grace of death, giving life its shape — the arc, the weight, the meaning that comes from having an end.

**Plane:** The Greylands / Plane of Death — a sanctuary where the dead can rest and, if they choose, begin again.  
**Influence:** The sanctity of death, rest, the cycle of rebirth  
**Note:** Perhaps the most benevolent of the Outer Gods, and the least understood.

*Counterpart among the New Gods: [[The New Gods#Moran|Moran]]*

## The Primordial Crucible

![[assets/outer-god-the-primordial-crucible.jpg]]
*"The First Builder · The Shattered One · The Four-In-One"*

The most powerful of the Outer Gods — the embodiment of all four elements simultaneously, **the creator of the material realm**. Built the universe and spent millennia trying to preserve the balance. Trusted the Eioran, and was betrayed.

**Planes:** Split across the Planes of Earth, Fire, Water, and Air  
**Influence:** Elemental power, the Marred, the Eioran Vaults  

### The Great Betrayal
The Eioran — the ancient proto-race who had fought to imprison the other gods — feared what the Crucible could do if it ever turned against them. Using power stolen from the imprisoned gods, they shattered the Crucible into four pieces before it could respond. The Crucible's last act was the creation of the [[The Marred|Marred]].

> *"The fear that they might one day reform drives more of the world's politics than most inhabitants know."*

**Current Status:** The fragments are still shattered across four elemental planes. Since approximately 3000 years ago, faint **resonance signals** have been detected passing between the planes — the fragments remembering each other. See [[campaign/Hidden Truths|Hidden Truths (DM Only)]].
