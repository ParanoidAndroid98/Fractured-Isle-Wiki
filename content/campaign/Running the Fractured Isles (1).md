# Running the Fractured Isles

#campaign #dm-guide

The Fractured Isles is a setting built on **consequence and complexity**. Every institution was built in the shadow of a previous catastrophe. Every ruler has an agenda shaped by what they've survived. The world rewards players who ask "why?" and punishes parties who assume the cleaner answer is the correct one.

---

## Core Tone

**Politics over combat.** The most significant conflicts in the Isles have been political before they were martial. The most powerful beings in the world are also the most institutionally embedded — you don't fight Umeial, you navigate what Umeial has built.

**Moral weight without moral simplicity.** The Chromatic Inquisition has genuinely saved thousands of lives through preemptive action. It has also imprisoned people for thought crimes. Both things are true and neither cancels the other.

**The past is present.** Events from eight thousand years ago have direct, traceable effects on the world of 3000 AF. Players who do the historical research will be rewarded.

**Gods exist and remain unclear.** The New Gods are demonstrably real. What they *want* is genuinely uncertain. Clergy who claim to know exactly what their god requires are, at best, extrapolating from fragmentary evidence.

---

## Factions & Campaign Seeds

### The Ebony Order vs. the Arcane Senate
The backbone of magical politics in the Isles. A party with members on both sides of this disagreement will find themselves in the middle of genuine debates with real stakes.

- What knowledge is too dangerous to exist?
- Who decides, and who watches the people who decide?
- The Order has been classifying materials at an increased rate since 2750 AF.

### The Undead Covenant
The covenant is real, entered into by consenting adults, the quality of life is measurably high. It is also entered into by people who are alive, about what happens when they are not.

- A living resident wants to leave and breach their covenant. How does the party respond?
- One of the undead houses is using remains in a way the covenant did not specify.

### The Marred Cults
Each of the four Marred attracts people who are, in themselves, sympathetic.

- Investigate a Bakujira cult: every member is someone who lost something irreplaceable.
- Respond to Kagutsuro cultists who are not violent and do not want to be saved.

### The Eioran Vaults
The most direct hook into the setting's deepest layer.

- A Vault appears nearby. The party is first on the scene, before the Order arrives.
- The artefact recovered is something the Order's registry suggests should not be possible.

## Campaign Escalation Path

**Tier 1 (Local):** Provincial politics, Marred cult investigation, Vault appearance response, Undead Lands covenant case.

**Tier 2 (Regional):** Inter-provincial conflict, Inquisition case crossing jurisdictions, Pathfinder expedition to anomalous territory.

**Tier 3 (Continental):** Marred convergence on multiple Vault sites, Outer God prison degradation becoming visible, Ysolde's final painting figures identified.

**Tier 4 (Cosmological):** See [[Hidden Truths]]. The party needs to have earned this layer.
