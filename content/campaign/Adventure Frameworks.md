# Adventure Frameworks

#campaign #adventures

Ready-to-run scenario outlines for the Fractured Isles. Each is written as a starting situation that can expand in multiple directions depending on player choices.

---

## Tier 1: Local Adventures

### The Vault at the Crossroads
**Hook:** A Vault emerges on a trade road between two provinces. The party is present. So are travellers, a merchant caravan, and within hours, an Ebony Order response team.

**The situation:** The Vault is open. Something inside has already been touched by one of the travellers — a gnome merchant who picked up an artefact and hasn't put it down since. The Order wants the Vault locked and the artefact transferred to their custody. The merchant doesn't want to give it up, and the artefact is doing something to their perception of time.

**What's actually happening:** The artefact is a minor Eioran sensory tool — it allows the user to see the resonance patterns the fragments are producing. The merchant is experiencing this as beautiful, overwhelming, and increasingly impossible to look away from. The Order knows what the tool does and wants it before the merchant starts describing what they're seeing out loud.

**Complications:**
- The merchant has a family in the next city who will want answers.
- A member of the Arcane Senate is in the caravan and believes the Senate has jurisdictional authority over Vault artefacts.
- The Order's response team leader is following orders she privately disagrees with.

### The Grief House
**Hook:** In a town in the [[nations/Black Dragon Province|Black Dragon Province]], a neighbourhood of twenty-three people has stopped leaving their homes. They are physically fine. They are simply... remembering. All the time. Past joys that feel more real than the present.

**The situation:** A Bakujira cult has been operating quietly here for two years. No dramatic rituals, no obvious coercion — just a support group for people who lost loved ones, which gradually shifted its therapeutic model toward helping members access past memories more vividly. The cultist leading the group is not malicious. They are themselves deeply grieving. The Marred's influence has been bleeding in through them.

**What's actually happening:** A minor Bakujira spawn — a Memory Eel — has been living in the neighbourhood's water supply and intensifying the effects of the cult's practices. The cult leader doesn't know. The twenty-three people in their homes are not in immediate danger, but the longer this continues the further they drift.

**Complications:**
- The cult leader will cooperate fully if approached with compassion, not at all if approached as a criminal.
- Three of the trapped residents are the families of [[organisations/The Chromatic Inquisition|Chromatic Inquisition]] officers who would very much prefer this to stay quiet.
- Removing the Memory Eel will end the enhanced state and leave twenty-three people with ordinary, unenhanced grief. Is that better?

### The Oath That Costs
**Hook:** A dwarven emissary approaches the party with a contract offer: escort them through [[nations/Green Dragon Province|Green Dragon Province]] territory to a specific location and ensure their safe return. Standard work. Good pay.

**The situation:** The location is a Vault site — one that opened 40 years ago and closed, but left residue the dwarves have tracked. What they want from the residue is not something they're willing to explain to non-dwarves. What they will say: it relates to a decision made during the Fracturing that the dwarven community has been carrying ever since.

**What's actually happening:** During the Fracturing, a dwarven ancestor used a Vault artefact to seal a rift that would otherwise have consumed a giant community. The artefact was a key — and using it as a plug meant it was spent. The dwarves want to determine whether the residue contains enough information to understand what was lost, and whether it can be recovered in any form.

**Complications:**
- The giant community that was saved knows this history. Their Grand Chieftain wants to be part of whatever recovery effort happens.
- The residue site is also a place where Frureonys's druids conduct private ceremonies. Access is not automatic.
- Something else is drawn to the residue site. It arrives the same night the party does.

## Tier 2: Regional Adventures

### The Inquisitor's Doubt
**Hook:** A senior [[organisations/The Chromatic Inquisition|Chromatic Inquisition]] officer contacts the party for unofficial assistance. Unofficial meaning: not through the Order, not reported to the Inquisitor General, completely deniable.

**The situation:** The Inquisitor has been assigned to monitor a seer who has been producing accurate short-range predictions in the [[nations/Copper Dragon Province|Copper Dragon Province]]. Standard protocol: ongoing observation, assessment of risk, potential detention if the predictions become destabilising. The Inquisitor has been following this protocol for six months. They have now realised the seer is predicting things that only an Inquisition informant would know.

**What's actually happening:** The seer is not predicting the future. They are receiving transmissions from [[nations/The Undead Lands|Ordo Spiritus]] — Vaelith Mournglass is testing a new long-range communication method and using the seer as an unwitting relay. The seer's predictions are accurate because they're repeating things Spiritus already knows.

**Complications:**
- Why is Spiritus feeding the seer Inquisition intelligence?
- The Inquisitor is right that this can't go through official channels — the informant is at a level where official inquiry would be stonewalled.
- The seer is genuinely developing low-level prophetic ability as a side effect of being used as a relay.

### The Province on the Edge
**Hook:** A long-running labour dispute in the [[nations/Copper Dragon Province|Copper Dragon Province]] is approaching a tipping point. The party is in the city when a significant confrontation occurs.

**The situation:** The artisan guilds and the patron class have been in negotiation for two years. The latest round collapsed. Someone has been funding the more radical faction on both sides — the most intransigent guild members and the most intransigent patrons both have new resources, new rhetoric, and new tactical advice that sounds like it comes from people who want the conflict to escalate rather than resolve.

**What's actually happening:** A financial instrument running through the [[organisations/The Banks|Gold Dragon Province banks]] is structured to pay out if the Copper Province's trade output drops below a certain threshold — a threshold it would hit in the event of a prolonged labour stoppage. Someone positioned this instrument two years ago, a month before the negotiations started.

**Complications:**
- Dask-Ijer knows something is being done to destabilise the province and hasn't been able to identify what.
- Both sides of the labour dispute have genuine grievances that predate the external manipulation.
- The instrument holder's identity is behind four layers of banking privacy law that the party would need either exceptional legal resources or exceptional illegal resources to pierce.

## Tier 3: Continental Adventures

### The Convergence
**Hook:** Three Vault emergences in seven days, in locations that form a triangle on the map. The Order responds to the first two. The party gets to the third first.

**The situation:** The interior of the third Vault contains something none of the Vault literature describes: a room where the walls are covered in Eioran script that is actively changing — writing and rewriting itself. At the room's centre, a device that is clearly activating.

**What's actually happening:** The Vault is responding to the Crucible resonance. The device is a message system — the Eioran built the Vaults with a trigger for exactly this situation, a way to communicate with whoever was present when the resonance reached a certain threshold. The message is addressed to "those who will face the choice we could not."

**The message:** The Eioran knew the Crucible might eventually try to reform. They built the Vaults partly as time capsules. The message explains what the Crucible actually was, what imprisoning it cost, and what they believe should happen when it reforms. Their recommendation may not be what the party expects.

**Complications:**
- The Order arrives while the party is reading the message. How much have they seen?
- One of the party members recognises a symbol in the Eioran script from their personal history.
- The device, having delivered its message, begins to shut down. The party has limited time before it closes.

### The Painting Comes True
**Hook:** A Pathfinder contacts the party urgently. They've compared their route map against the locations in Ysolde's final painting — the ones visible in the background landscape — and they're standing in one of them.

**The situation:** The Pathfinder is Figure 3 in the painting. They know this because they obtained an illegal reproduction of the painting three years ago from a contact in the Brass Library, and they've been slowly realising it ever since. They have come to the party because one of the party members is also in the painting — and the scene depicted in the painting is going to happen in this location, and they need to decide what to do about it.

**What's actually happening:** The painting doesn't show the end of the world. It shows a decision point. Five people standing at a convergence site, with four elemental tears behind them — the planes visible through the breaches. The title *The End of All* refers to the end of one age, not the destruction of existence. What the five figures are deciding is what comes next.

**Complications:**
- The Inquisition is tracking the Pathfinder — they identified Figure 3 months ago.
- Figure 5, the one Ximoal won't name, arrives at the location. The party now knows who they are.
- The elemental tears in the painting begin to appear in the sky above the location.
