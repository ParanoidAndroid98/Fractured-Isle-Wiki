# Calendar & Festivals

#faith #calendar #festivals

The calendar of the Fractured Isles divides the year into **eight months of 45 days each**, with five additional days at the year's end known collectively as the **Days of Fracture** — a period when the asteroid belt reaches its lowest visible point and the planes are considered at their most permeable.

---

## The Eight Months

### First Month: Ashwake
**Patron:** [[cosmology/The New Gods#Kindra|Kindra]]  
**Major Festival:** The Turning (dedicated to [[faith/Saints#Saint Loujar|Saint Loujar]])

Represents the burning away of what is no longer needed and the courage to face what comes next. Children are formally named or renamed. Adults who have decided to leave behind a version of themselves make public commitments to that change — new names, changed appearances, new vocations. Old possessions are burned; the smoke is considered a message sent forward to who you are becoming.

*Provincial Festival — [[nations/Red Dragon Province|Red Dragon Province]] — Festival of Embers:* Inventors showcase creations throughout Ashwake. Some explode. The explosions are considered part of the programme.

---

### Second Month: Flowtide
**Patron:** [[cosmology/The New Gods#Vapos|Vapos]]  
**Major Festival:** The First Voyage

Represents trade, travel, and the movement of ideas. Boats are blessed, merchants exchange gifts with trading partners, new commercial agreements are formally announced. The festival has expanded from a maritime tradition into a general celebration of all forms of exchange — wagon routes, postal services, the Pathfinders' annual map publication.

---

### Third Month: Bloomrise
**Patron:** [[cosmology/The New Gods#Vitasaol|Vitasaol]]  
**Major Festival:** The Great Bloom (dedicated to [[faith/Saints#Saint Varelis|Saint Varelis]])

Represents spring and fertility in the broadest sense. Enormous communal feasts, formal planting ceremonies, the highest concentration of weddings of any month in the year. Offerings are left at forest edges as acknowledgement of the land's generosity.

*Provincial Festival — [[nations/Green Dragon Province|Green Dragon Province]] — First Harvest:* Food freely shared throughout the province. No one is allowed to go hungry. The enforcement of this principle is gentle and absolute.

---

### Fourth Month: Stoneheart
**Patron:** [[cosmology/The New Gods#Theran|Theran]]  
**Major Festival:** The Day of the Giants (dedicated to [[faith/Saints#Saint Bricwar|Saint Bricwar]])

Represents resilience, endurance, and the strength to hold your position when everything argues for retreat. Tournaments, tests of strength, and smithing competitions run throughout the month. New knights and soldiers take their formal oaths. Competitions are open to all regardless of class or origin.

*Provincial Festival — [[nations/Blue Dragon Province|Blue Dragon Province]] — Oathday:* Citizens publicly renew personal vows. The military parades are the most disciplined in the Isles.

---

### Fifth Month: Starveil
**Patron:** [[organisations/The Ebony Order|Tothiri]] ([[faith/Saints#Saint Tothiri|Saint Tothiri]])  
**Major Festival:** Secret Study

Represents the acquisition of knowledge and the mysteries of the arcane. Universities open to the public, libraries hold their most ambitious programming, arcane discoveries of the preceding year are presented to general audiences. The tradition of writing secrets and burning them is the festival's most widely practised element.

*Provincial Festival — [[nations/Bronze Dragon Province|Bronze Dragon Province]] — Arcane Symposium:* Every major magical institution sends delegates. Described as a mixture of academic conference and magical disaster in approximately equal proportion.

---

### Sixth Month: Dreamfall
**Patron:** [[cosmology/The New Gods#Neithona|Neithona]]  
**Major Festival:** The Night of Veils (dedicated to [[faith/Saints#Saint Ysolde|Saint Ysolde]])

Represents destiny, imagination, and the dissolution of boundaries that usually keep different kinds of people apart. Everyone wears masks. Noble and poor mingle without distinction. Dream journals are shared. Fortunes are read. Theatrical performances during Dreamfall are the most ambitious of the year, frequently addressing subjects too politically sensitive at other times.

*Provincial Festival — [[nations/Copper Dragon Province|Copper Dragon Province]] — Grand Masquerade:* The most famous festival in the Isles. Week-long event involving art, music, theatre, and costuming of extraordinary ambition. Provincial attendance swells threefold.

*Provincial Festival — [[nations/Brass Dragon Province|Brass Dragon Province]] — Festival of Questions:* Children permitted to ask scholars anything. Public philosophical debates run continuously. Most intellectually stimulating provincial festival by general consensus.

---

### Seventh Month: Silver Rest
**Patron:** [[cosmology/The New Gods#Moran|Moran]]  
**Major Festival:** The Night of Lanterns (dedicated to [[faith/Saints#Saint Celzala|Saint Celzala]])

Considered the most sacred night of the year by most faiths. Represents remembrance and the tending of the relationship between the living and the dead. Families remember those who have died. Communities march through streets dressed as the dead, floating lanterns down rivers as messages to the Greylands. The silvery lights that appear during the festival — their source never satisfactorily explained — are widely understood as Celzala's acknowledgement.

*Provincial Festival — [[nations/Silver Dragon Province|Silver Dragon Province]] — Winterfire:* Entire communities gather around enormous bonfires. Storytelling, communal meals, and the singing tradition that exists nowhere else in the Isles at this scale.

*Provincial Festival — [[nations/Black Dragon Province|Black Dragon Province]] — Vigil of Bones:* A candlelight ceremony in which living residents formally thank the ancestors whose remains now serve the city.

---

### Eighth Month: Goldend
**Patron:** [[cosmology/The New Gods#Shangotha|Shangotha]]  
**Major Festival:** The Reckoning

Represents law, civic life, and the renewal of social contracts. Public review of the laws, courthouses open to public observation, formal renewal of judicial and political oaths. The month ends with fireworks and public celebration — the year ending with the acknowledgement that its laws and structures have been examined and found sufficient, or amended to become so.

*Provincial Festival — [[nations/Gold Dragon Province|Gold Dragon Province]] — Counting Day:* Markets, auctions, and trade fairs. Economic predictions for the coming year presented publicly. Treated as entertainment by most, scripture by a few.

---

## The Days of Fracture
*Five days at the year's end, belonging to everyone and to no time*

The asteroid belt sits lowest at this period, filling the night sky with reflected light. The planes are considered at their most accessible. Observed with unusual solemnity even by the secular.

| Day | Name | Observance |
|-----|------|-----------|
| 1 | **The Betrayal** | Remembrance of the Outer Gods and the war that shaped creation. A day of quiet reflection and history, not celebration. |
| 2 | **The Shattering** | Remembrance of the splitting of the Primordial Crucible. Prayer that the fearful day of reunification never comes. Marred cults traditionally become most active — which keeps the Inquisition occupied. |
| 3 | **The Day of Sacrifice** | Celebration of the New Gods and re-enactments of their founding acts. The most theatrically elaborate day, with religious ceremonies in every province. |
| 4 | **Saints Eve** | Celebration of all saints and the humbling thought that within any lifetime, someone present might join their number. Shrines throughout the Isles see their highest attendance. |
| 5 | **The New Dawn** | The most joyful of the five days — celebration of the year ending and hope for what comes next. Fireworks, gatherings, the exchange of small gifts prepared throughout Goldend. |
