# Nomadic Lands of the Giants

#nations #giants #nomadic

![[assets/nomadic-lands-giants-intro.jpg]]

The lands of the giants are not empty. They are full of people who have decided that **the most honest relationship with a landscape is one of continuous movement**, and who have built their entire material culture around making that movement as rich and as connected as it possibly can be.

---

## Overview

The rolling hills and great plains of the giant territories stretch across the interior of the continent, dotted with **moving villages** — clusters of wheeled and portable structures that travel predictable seasonal routes, stopping at known waypoints, exchanging goods and news with other villages at the intersections.

The giant artistic tradition is primarily **large-scale environmental**: stone arrangements, land carving, living plant architecture, sound sculptures designed to resonate with specific winds. The landscape of the giant territories looks, from above, like a very long and very considered artistic statement.

---

## Government

**Grand Chieftain Solanna Stonewalker** governs by consensus — which, with a nomadic population spread across thousands of miles, requires an **annual gathering at the Plains Convergence** where representatives of all village councils meet to resolve disputes and set policy.

Solanna's authority derives from demonstrated wisdom rather than inherited title. The previous Grand Chieftain served for two hundred and thirty years. The one before that, forty. Tenure is not guaranteed.

## Grand Chieftain Solanna Stonewalker

![[assets/giant-grand-chieftain-solanna.jpg]]

A vast giant whose **glowing scars** — earned in the Fracturing, the heritage of [[faith/Saints#Saint Bricwar|Saint Bricwar's]] lineage — cover their body like a map of every battle fought to close the rifts. The scars are luminous at night. Solanna dresses as a nomad, which is not performance: they travel the routes with the village councils rather than maintaining a fixed seat of power.

## Cultural Notes

The giant communities attract artists and wanderers from across the Isles who come for a season and often stay. The freedom of the nomadic life has a genuine pull, and the giant tradition of welcoming those who demonstrate genuine engagement with the community has produced a cultural mixing unusual among the provinces.

The **Pathfinders of Aestri** maintain their headquarters — the Waystation — in the Nomadic Lands.
