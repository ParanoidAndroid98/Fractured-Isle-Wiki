# Green Dragon Province

#nations #dragon-province #green-province

> *"The forest does not owe anyone its resources. Those who take from it are guests who should behave accordingly."*

**Provincial Dragon:** Ancient Green Dragon **Frureonys**  
**Capital:** Curansaltus  
**Economy:** Deep spiritual trade, Druidic Sanctuary, ecological expertise

---

## Overview

The last truly wild woodland in the Fractured Isles — a forest so old that trees at its heart **predate the Fracturing**, and so vast its full extent has never been completely mapped. The people who live here are not separate from the forest. They are **part of it** — the wood elves who serve as its primary guardians have spent centuries learning to work with the forest's own systems rather than impose different ones.

The province is deeply spiritual in a way that other provinces generally are not. The spirits of the forest are real here in the same way that stones and weather are real — not supernatural but natural, present, responsive, and in possession of a very long memory.

---

## History

The **Purified Garden** (~9600 AB completion) is the defining act of the province's history — a concentration of planar corruption left over from the Fracturing that the wood elves spent **four centuries and twelve years** cleansing. No other province undertook a comparable act of sustained sacrifice for the benefit of the entire world. The green province does not advertise this. It does not need to.

The alliance with the [[nations/Silver Dragon Province|Silver Dragon Province]] that began with the Frozen Famine has been the province's most significant long-term political alliance — two cultures with very little in common on the surface, united by one specific winter of generosity.

## Major Settlements

![[province-green-city.jpg]]

**Curansaltus** *(Capital)*  
A canopy city built above the forest floor, the buildings growing from and with the ancient trees rather than standing on them. Seat of both provincial government and the Druidic Sanctuary. Population ~120,000 — the lowest of any provincial capital, and considered by its residents entirely appropriate.

**The Wicker Wilds**  
A settlement at the forest's edge that serves as the primary point of contact between the province and outside visitors. Many who come intending to stay briefly do not leave.

## Provincial Dragon: Frureonys

![[dragon-frureonys.jpg|assets/dragon-dask-ijer.jpg]]

**Humanoid Form:** Brown-skinned, ten feet tall, flowers woven through hair that moves of its own accord, bark-like skin texture, dressed in living plant material that continues growing and changing. Moves with absolute deliberateness.

**Personality:** Unhurried, deeply attentive, willing to engage with any argument on its merits, and completely inflexible on the question of ecological preservation. These things coexist without contradiction.  
**Greatest Achievement:** The Purified Garden. It took four hundred and twelve years.  
**Greatest Regret:** The settlements that were moved to preserve the ecology. The decision was correct. The cost to individuals was real.

**Current Plot Hooks:**
- Something old is waking in the deepest part of the forest, and Frureonys has not yet decided whether it is a threat or a return.
- A civilisation on the province's eastern border is expanding, and the expansion has reached the forest line.
- The Druidic Sanctuary has closed to outside visitors for the first time in eight hundred years.

## Provincial Festival
**First Harvest** (during [[faith/Calendar and Festivals#Third Month Bloomrise|Bloomrise]]): Food freely shared throughout the province. No one is allowed to go hungry. The enforcement of this principle is gentle and absolute.
