# Age of Expansion — 1500 to 5200 AB

#history #age-of-expansion

> *Previous: [[Age of Foundations]] · Next: [[Age of Law]]*

As the world recovered and populations grew, the ancient races began to build in earnest. This was the age that produced the great elven cities — genuinely as magnificent as legend says, before they became instruments of empire — and the first draconic centres of learning. It also produced the quiet miracle of human civilisation, which spread across every terrain the Isles offered within a thousand years of first appearance.

---

## The Ancient Races Build

### The Dragons
Established the first great centres of arcane learning — not schools in any institutional sense, but gatherings of knowledge: **libraries carved into mountainsides** and sealed with magical protections that some still hold today. The draconic approach to knowledge was possessive and careful, born from awareness of what uncontrolled arcane power had done to the Eioran.

### The Elves
Built their golden cities, which in this period were genuinely as magnificent as legend says, and had not yet become the instruments of empire they would later turn into. The elven cities of the Expansion are remembered with a particular melancholy by the oldest wood elves and dark elves — a golden age whose trajectory was already, to anyone who looked carefully, pointing toward the Fracturing.

### The Giants
Continued their nomadic role as **protectors and reconstructors** — intervening where cataclysms threatened to undo the reconstruction work of the Foundations age, reshaping terrain when natural disasters opened new wounds. In this period they became less emergency responders and more wandering guardians, travelling routes that took them through the territories of the newer races regularly enough to know them.

---

## The Younger Races Flourish

### Humanity's Unexpected Rise
Humans demonstrated a capacity for social organisation and cultural transmission that seemed to outpace their individual lifespans — **knowledge and technique passing from generation to generation with remarkable fidelity**. Within a thousand years of their first appearance, human settlements were everywhere, adapting to every terrain the Isles offered.

No other race expanded as quickly or as broadly. The ancient races watched with a mixture of admiration and unease. The dragons, who measured in centuries, found it difficult to fully reckon with a people who remade their understanding of the world every three generations.

### The Dwarves Go Deep
The dwarves spent much of the Expansion age in the deep places — expanding their under-mountain networks, discovering the mineral seams the Crucible had laid down, developing the craft traditions that would make them irreplaceable to the surface world's economy. Their contact with the surface races was deliberate and limited, managed on dwarven terms.

---

## The Wildheart Migration

The great movement of the aarakocra peoples across the entire width of the Isles dates to the later part of this era, though the exact period is contested. The aarakocra themselves **give no more explanation than that it was time to spread**, and do not regard this as unusual.

The cities and territories they vacated during the migration are some of the most archaeologically rich sites in the known world, though accessing them requires climbing ability and a tolerance for vertiginous exposure.

Scholars have debated the cause for centuries. The most widely accepted theory — that the Wildheart Migration was a response to increased planar pressure in the territories the aarakocra left — has the uncomfortable implication that the aarakocra sensed something approaching long before anyone else did.

## The Shadow Over the Age

In retrospect, the Expansion contains the seeds of every crisis that followed. The elves' growing belief in their own organisational superiority. The dragons' debate over whether to share knowledge — a debate whose resolution required two centuries of war. The human expansion that the ancient races welcomed without fully reckoning with what it would mean for their own political dominance.

It was also an age of genuine beauty and genuine accomplishment. The Expansion built most of what survives of the pre-Fracturing world. The forests Varelis's ancestors planted. The dwarven deep-roads. The first roads across the giant territories. Most of what the modern Isles inherits from before the Fracturing comes from this age — both the beauty and the hubris.
