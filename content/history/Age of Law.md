# Age of Law — 5200 to 8300 AB

#history #age-of-law

> *Previous: [[Age of Expansion]] · Next: [[Age of Saints]]*

The age in which the world's first great institutions were built — out of necessity, out of catastrophe, and out of the hard-won understanding that power without structure is simply a different kind of chaos. The Concord of Seven is the document that created the world the Fractured Isles inhabits today, more than any other single event. It was built on the bones of a two-hundred-year war.

---

## The Draconic Infraction — 5200 to 5400 AB

The first great crisis of the settled world arrived not from outside but from within the dragon community. The question that divided them: **should the younger races be given access to arcane magic?**

This seems almost quaint in retrospect, given that magic now suffuses every institution in the Isles. At the time, it was a question about the nature of power and who deserved to wield it. It divided the dragons into factions that fought **a two-hundred-year war of duels and assassinations**. The battlefields of the Draconic Infraction are still magically tainted — the residue of dragon-scale sorcery absorbed into the rock over centuries.

The provinces visible in this scarring:
- The **Onyx Mountains** between the Black Dragon Province and the Undead Lands formed from the geological impact of concentrated dragon sorcery
- The tainted zones in the [[nations/Blue Dragon Province|Blue Dragon Province]]'s northern reaches
- The residual wild magic zones that still exist in parts of the [[nations/Bronze Dragon Province|Bronze Dragon Province]]

### The Sacrifice of Aravoss
The war's resolution came not from diplomacy but from sacrifice. The elder dragon **Aravoss** chose to use their own body to seal away the most dangerous magics that had been unleashed during the conflict — wrapping themselves around a mountain range and **petrifying, forever**, to prevent those energies from dispersing into the world.

What remains of Aravoss can be seen in the Red Dragon Province to this day: a colossal stone figure, perfectly preserved, still radiating a faint warmth. The [[locations/Landmark Locations#The Frozen Wing|Frozen Wing]] is sacred across every dragon tradition as evidence that the most powerful thing a being with sufficient magical ability can do is use that ability to protect rather than dominate.

## The Concord of Seven — 6100 AB

The Concord grew in two stages: the informal agreements of **5350 AB** that ended the active conflict, and the formal **Concord of Seven** of 6100 AB that established lasting institutional frameworks.

The Concord of Seven established:
- The **arcane schools** as they exist today, with structured curricula and licensing requirements
- The framework for **magical law** across all provinces
- The answer to the original question: magic would be **shared with all races, within limits, under oversight**
- The precursor institutions to both the [[organisations/The Arcane Senate|Arcane Senate]] and the [[organisations/The Ebony Order|Ebony Order]]

> *"The Concord of Seven is the document that created the world the Fractured Isles inhabits now, more than any other single event."*

The seven signatories were dragons representing the major factional positions of the Infraction. The document's genius was in creating enough institutional structure that future disagreements had somewhere to go other than into open conflict.

## The Ferrite Rebellion — 7000 AB

The dwarves' version of the same argument: **tradition against progress, the value of ancient craft against the demand of a changing world**. It was fought in great underground fortresses with rune engines and siege machinery across the deep-road networks beneath the dwarven island.

The causes:
- A faction within dwarven society demanding the adoption of arcane-enhanced manufacturing techniques from the surface world
- A conservative faction insisting on the primacy of pure craft and the spiritual significance of working without arcane assistance
- Economic pressures from surface competition that made the argument urgent

The resolution: the **Forged Accords** — a settlement that allowed both sides to claim victory and that shaped dwarven culture into the **balance of deep conservatism and fierce innovation** that characterises it today. The Forged Accords created the dual-track system in which traditional craft and arcane-enhanced craft are both honoured, practised in different contexts, and judged by different standards.

[[faith/Saints#Saint Korden|Saint Korden]] emerged from the turbulence following the Ferrite Rebellion — a dwarven heir who chose justice over inheritance in the difficult period of reconstruction that followed.

---

## The Crystal Plague — 8450 AB

A magical disease emerging from residue concentrated in the poorer districts of several major cities — the long tail of the Draconic Infraction's arcane contamination, finally finding a biological vector. Its effects were varied and terrible: progressive crystallisation of skin and joints, uncontrolled chitinous growth, episodes of violent mania.

**[[faith/Saints#Saint Merrow|Saint Merrow]]** spent the Crystal Plague years treating the sick across multiple provinces as their own body slowly crystallised, and was lifted to sainthood by [[cosmology/The New Gods#Vapos|Vapos]] as the cure was finally achieved — the only recorded instance of a New God taking direct physical action in the material world.

## The Arcane Uprising — 8200 AB

Tothiri's greatest student, a half-giant called **Cadrin**, became convinced that their master was too cautious — that the forbidden magics being locked away by the emerging Ebony Order could be used to end hunger and protect the world from future cataclysm.

They were not wrong about the power of those magics. They were catastrophically wrong about their ability to control them.

The arcane prison Cadrin built — intended to contain the most dangerous knowledge — **became the first facility of the Ebony Order's detention arm, and Cadrin its first prisoner**. They remain there, as far as anyone knows.

This event prompted the formal establishment of both the Ebony Order and the Arcane Senate in their modern forms.

---

## Foundation of the Ebony Order and Arcane Senate — 8300–8350 AB

See: [[organisations/The Ebony Order]], [[organisations/The Arcane Senate]]

[[faith/Saints#Saint Tothiri|Saint Tothiri]] founded the Ebony Order in 8300 AB. The Arcane Senate followed in 8350 AB as the civilian oversight counterpart. The institutional tension between these two bodies — the Order's conservatism against the Senate's belief in wider access — was built into their structure from the first day and has not resolved in ten thousand years.
