## Origins

The banking infrastructure of the Fractured Isles began as a practical solution to a specific problem: **dragon hoards**. The great dragons accumulated wealth in physical form — gold, gems, worked metal — and the problem of moving significant quantities across the Isles was logistically impossible.

The [[nations/Gold Dragon Province|Gold Dragon Province]] developed the first system of **promissory notes** — documents that could be redeemed at any Gold Province facility for the equivalent in physical wealth from the local hoard. The modern banking system grew from there.

---

## Current State

Banks are present in every major city in the Isles. The notes they issue have become the **primary medium of large-scale trade**, backed by the collective wealth of the dragon hoards rather than any single treasury. This makes the system extraordinarily stable — dragon hoards do not deplete in the way mortal treasuries do.

This gives the Gold Dragon Province a degree of financial influence that is, technically, separate from political power — and that in practice cannot be entirely distinguished from it.

---

## The TideLock Agreement

The [[organisations/Pathfinders of Aestri|TideLock Agreement]] that stabilised the shipping lanes after a period of piracy and trade disruption was negotiated partly on the infrastructure the banks had created. Many provisions that made it work depended on financial instruments only the banking system could provide.

- **Mediators:** Vapos's clergy
- **Guarantor:** The Gold Province
- **Status:** Has held for over a thousand years.
