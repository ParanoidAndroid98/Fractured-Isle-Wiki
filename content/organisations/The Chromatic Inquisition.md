# The Chromatic Inquisition

![[assets/organisation-chromatic-inquisition.jpg]]

#organisations #chromatic-inquisition

**Founded:** ~8400 AB (mandate formalised post-Veiled Prophet crisis)  
**Jurisdiction:** All provinces by treaty; above provincial law in arcane and religious matters  
**Leadership:** The High Chromatic — currently **Inquisitor General Pelara Voss**

---

## Purpose

The Chromatic Inquisition exists because not every problem can wait for a trial, and not every trial can happen in a courtroom. They are the Isles' **travelling judiciary** — investigators, judges, and where necessary executioners — addressing the specific category of problem that provincial governments cannot handle: arcane crime, religious dispute, and matters that cross jurisdictional lines in ways conventional law was not designed to manage.

They work closely with the [[The Ebony Order|Ebony Order]] and are often mistaken for a branch of it. They are not. The distinction: the Order concerns itself with knowledge and artifacts; the Inquisition concerns itself with **people and power**.

---

## The Hidden Mandate

What the general public does not know — and what the Inquisition's rank and file mostly do not know either — is that the organisation has a **secondary mandate that predates most of its official functions**. The Inquisition was established, in part, to track the movements of the [[cosmology/The Marred|Marred]] and monitor the status of the elemental prisons.

The fear that the four corrupted entities could be used as instruments to reform the [[cosmology/The Outer Gods#The Primordial Crucible|Primordial Crucible]] is the oldest open question in the Order's files. The Inquisition's senior leadership are the custodians of that question and have been for three thousand years.

## The Veiled Prophet Case

Taught at every level of Inquisition training as the **foundational example** of why prophetic ability requires institutional management. A being who can accurately predict future crimes can prevent enormous suffering — and can also construct an apparatus of preemptive enforcement indistinguishable, from the outside, from tyranny.

The Inquisition's current protocols around seers and prophets are detailed, nuanced, and the result of the Veiled Prophet situation not having ended particularly well for anyone involved.

## Notable Cases & Doctrine

- **The Veiled Prophet (9100 AB):** Changeling prophet capable of predicting disasters. Disappeared after the Inquisition's interventions. Prompted the formal development of seer protocols.
- **[[faith/Saints#Saint Ysolde|Saint Ysolde]]:** The painter-prophet whose disagreements with the Inquisition were never resolved before their disappearance in 9200 AB.
- **[[cosmology/The Marred#Fūjin-no-Kage Shadow of the Wind|Fūjin-no-Kage]] Cults:** Considered among the most urgent interventions undertaken, as victims are by definition unable to ask for help.
