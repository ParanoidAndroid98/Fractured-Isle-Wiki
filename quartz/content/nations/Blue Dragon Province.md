## Overview

Fortress cities connected by river gorges cutting through mountain ranges. The people here are not martial in the way the Red Province is martial — they value **strength as integrity**: the question is not "what can you do?" but "what will you hold to when it costs you something?"

**Truth** is the province's central cultural value, practised with a rigour that outsiders sometimes find confrontational. The Blue Province does not have strong social conventions around comfortable dishonesty. People say what they think, ask what they want to know, and expect to be held to what they commit to.

---

## History

The Blue Province's history is largely the history of its relationship with the [[organisations/The Ebony Order|Ebony Order]], which chose to establish its Grand Library here in recognition of the province's existing scholarly infrastructure and the natural security of the mountain fortresses. The Library has been part of the province's cultural life for over eight thousand years.

The one-eyed scar marking Ancient Kentath's humanoid form dates from the Draconic Infraction — Kentath fought on the side of restricted access and lost the argument as well as the eye. The decision to join the Concord afterward was, by their own account, not reluctant. They had been wrong. The Concord was the correct response.

---

## Government

Governed by a **council of Wardens** — senior figures drawn from military, academic, and religious institutions — who advise Kentath and administer day-to-day functions. The dragon's direct authority is reserved for matters of war, treaty, and Ebony Order relations. Everything else runs through the council by design — Kentath believes good governance requires people with more current information than an ancient dragon in a high fortress necessarily has.

---

## Culture & Society

The martial tradition of the Blue Province is a tradition of **martial arts rather than martial arms** — the development of body and will as instruments of discipline and clarity. The most prestigious combat form resembles a flowing unarmed practice more than conventional fighting. Practitioners are formidable. The form is also practised by people who have no intention of ever fighting anyone, as a way of developing stillness and focus.

The province has produced **the most rigorous critiques of Inquisition overreach** in the historical record. The distinction between order that serves people and order that serves itself is taken seriously here.

## Major Settlements
![[province-blue-city.jpg]]

**The Azure Spire** *(Capital)*  
Built around the mountain from which it takes its name, the city rises in tiers from the river valley to the peak, with the Ebony Order Grand Library at the summit. Population ~180,000.

**Thalrothar**  
The province's primary military academy city, where both provincial soldiers and Inquisition candidates train alongside each other in a relationship that is, the faculty insist, entirely healthy.

**Vyrmguard**  
A heavily fortified border city originally built to watch the province's approaches, now primarily a trade hub and the site of the most significant annual martial arts tournament in the Isles.

## Provincial Dragon: Kentath

![[assets/dragon-kentath.jpg]]

**Humanoid Form:** Tall, blue-haired, lean like a martial artist. Visible scarring from the Infraction, four horns, yellow draconic eyes — the left eye clouded and blind from a wound received during the Draconic Infraction.

**Personality:** Precise, honest to a degree that can be uncomfortable, deeply principled, capable of genuine warmth to those who demonstrate integrity.  
**Philosophy:** True strength is the ability to change your mind when the evidence demands it.  
**Greatest Achievement:** The establishment of the Concord — specifically, persuading several dragons who had fought against him to agree to it anyway.  
**Greatest Regret:** The years during the Infraction when they were entirely certain they were right. The certainty itself, not the position.

**Current Plot Hooks:**
- Kentath has begun reviewing a piece of historical legislation that has been settled for three thousand years.
- Someone in the Order's Grand Library has been accessing restricted sections without triggering the security systems, which should be impossible.

## Provincial Festival
**Oathday** (during [[faith/Calendar and Festivals#Fourth Month Stoneheart|Stoneheart]]): Citizens publicly renew personal vows. The military parades are the most disciplined in the Isles.
