## Overview

Home to the greatest medical knowledge in the Fractured Isles, the most sophisticated necromantic research, and the **liveliest ongoing debate about what the relationship between those two things should be**.

The province does not look the way outsiders expect. The cities are architecturally striking — dark stone and high arches, enclosed spaces that retain warmth, illumination designed for those who are unwell. The people are not morbid. They have, as a population, **an unusually comfortable relationship with death and with the dead** — which, in a province that borders the Undead Lands and does significant research with undead subjects, is probably adaptive.

---

## History

The founding was closely tied to the establishment of the [[nations/The Undead Lands|Undead Dominion]] next door. Zirdirenth saw the potential in a relationship between the province's healing expertise and the undead's unique capacity to serve as research subjects for work that would be unethical with living patients — not because the undead could not be harmed, but because harm to the undead and harm to the living were different categories.

The agreement struck between Zirdirenth and the four undead houses produced the province's most significant ethical framework and its most significant ethical disputes. The consent protocols developed here are the most detailed in the Isles. Whether they are sufficient is a question the province's ethics commission has been examining since the second century AF.

---

## Government

A **democracy of expertise**: the governing council is elected by credentialed practitioners, with seats proportional to the size of each medical and research speciality. Zirdirenth participates as a permanent adviser with veto authority over treaty matters, but has used that veto twice in three thousand years.

---

## Culture & Society

Conversation in the Black Province tends toward the direct and the precise. Imprecise language about conditions, causes, and consequences generates mild social discomfort. The flip side: a **remarkable culture of acknowledgement** — of error, of uncertainty, of the limits of current knowledge. The phrase "we don't know yet" is heard more here than anywhere in the Isles, and it is not considered an admission of failure.

Dark elves are common throughout the province, working as healers, death-bed companions, and researchers.

## Major Settlements

![[assets/province-black-city.jpg]]

**Crono Salvis** *(Capital)*  
"Salvation in time" — an aspiration and a pressure simultaneously. Built around the Shanadol Sanctum at its centre. Population ~160,000.

**Golgotha**  
Originally a military outpost guarding the Onyx Mountains pass, now a mixed settlement with a large dark elf community and the province's primary facility for necromantic medicine research.

**Dusk Phane**  
A coastal city with the province's largest general hospital, known for treating cases that other provincial facilities cannot manage.

## Provincial Dragon: Zirdirenth

![[assets/dragon-zirdirenth.jpg]]

**Humanoid Form:** Dark-skinned woman, short black hair, habitually gaunt expression, medical clothes tailored to look noble. One arm replaced by a mechanical and necromantic prosthetic, lost during the Fracturing.

**Personality:** Methodical, honest about the limits of her knowledge, genuinely invested in the province's work, occasionally capable of a dry humour that surprises people who have read too much into the gaunt expression.  
**Philosophy:** Progress requires making mistakes and acknowledging them.  
**Greatest Achievement:** The ethics framework governing undead research consent — not because it is perfect, but because it is the first genuine attempt to engage with the question.  
**Greatest Regret:** Several specific research subjects from the first century AF who consented to procedures she would not permit today.

**Current Plot Hooks:**
- A research project in Golgotha has produced results that the council ethics committee has placed under review. The nature of the review is not public.
- The Onyx Mountains pass has been showing signs of unusual activity.

## Provincial Festival
**Vigil of Bones** (during [[faith/Calendar and Festivals#Seventh Month Silver Rest|Silver Rest]]): A candlelight ceremony in which living residents formally thank the ancestors whose remains now serve the city. More moving than it sounds.
