# Cosmology of the Fractured Isles

#cosmology

![[assets/world-map.jpg]]

The universe was shaped by the war between primordial forces before creation had fixed form. That war left scars — in the architecture of the planes, in the blood of every living thing, and in the prisons that keep the old powers from finishing what they started.

---

## The Three Tiers of Divinity

### [[The Outer Gods]]
The original forces of existence — embodiments of law, chaos, life, death, and nature itself — who warred before the universe took shape. Each is now **imprisoned within a plane that mirrors their own nature**. Their influence still bleeds through. Their cults still find them.

→ [[The Outer Gods]]

### [[The New Gods]]
The transformed souls of the [[history/Age of Foundations|Eioran]] — the first mortal race — who dissolved their physical forms into the fabric of reality after the [[history/Age of Foundations#The Great Betrayal|Great Betrayal]]. They are not omnipotent. They communicate through the world itself: weather, dreams, and the granting of [[faith/Saints|sainthood]].

→ [[The New Gods]]

### [[The Marred]]
Four corrupted elemental entities — the last act of the [[The Outer Gods#The Primordial Crucible|Primordial Crucible]] before it was shattered. They are not gods and not demons. They are wounds in the material plane that have learned to walk.

→ [[The Marred]]

## The Great Betrayal (Mayra Proddare)
*See [[history/Age of Foundations#The Great Betrayal|The Great Betrayal]]*

The Eioran fought alongside the [[The Outer Gods#The Primordial Crucible|Primordial Crucible]] to imprison the other Outer Gods. Then, afraid of the Crucible's power, they used stolen divine energy to **shatter it into four elemental fragments**. The Crucible's last act was creating the [[The Marred|Marred]]. The Eioran's guilt transformed them into the [[The New Gods|New Gods]].

> *"The New Gods are the Eioran. They remember what they did. It shapes everything about how they exercise their power — with restraint, with uncertainty, and with a respect for mortal choice that the Outer Gods never showed."*
