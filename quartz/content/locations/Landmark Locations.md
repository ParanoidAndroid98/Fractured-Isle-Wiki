# Landmark Locations

#locations

![[world-map.jpg]]

The Fractured Isles is defined as much by its extraordinary places as by its peoples. The following are the most significant landmarks in the known world — sites that have shaped history, carry secrets, or possess what the giant tradition calls **"weight"**: the sense that a place has seen enough that the seeing has left a mark.

---

## [[The Cracked Crown]]

![[assets/landmark-cracked-crown.jpg]]
**Location:** Silver Dragon Province, northern territories  
**Type:** Ruin / Wild Magic Zone  
What remains of Celeborn's elven capital city — towers cracked but still standing, saturated with wild magic that moves in patterns researchers have been studying for three thousand years without reaching consensus on what they mean. At its centre, the throne room stands intact, the throne itself appearing to **grow very slowly upward** through the cracked ceiling.

---

## [[The Sunken Maw]]

![[assets/landmark-sunken-maw.jpg]]
**Location:** Southern seas, ~200 miles off the Silver Dragon Province coast  
**Type:** Geographical Anomaly / Point of Origin  
A permanent whirlpool of impossible scale, ringed by tooth-like rock formations that move when approached. Produces a sound at low tide described as "the sound of something large breathing." The place where the Marred are said to have emerged. The Ebony Order maintains a monitoring station three miles from the outer edge and declines to discuss its findings.

---

## [[The First Fire]]

![[assets/landmark-first-fire.jpg]]
**Location:** Red Dragon Province, adjacent to Emberhold Bastion  
**Type:** Sacred Site / Active Volcano  
The volcano Kindra is said to have used as their forge when the world was young. Burns in colours standard volcanic chemistry does not produce. Pilgrimages here are among the most significant acts of devotion in Kindra's faith. The Order's arcane sensors detect something in the crater — and cannot characterise what they detect.

---

## [[The Frozen Wing]]
![[landmark-frozen-wing.jpg]]
**Location:** Red Dragon Province, northern Scarlet Mountains  
**Type:** Sacred Site / Geological Feature  
The petrified body of **Aravoss**, the ancient dragon who chose to seal the most dangerous magics of the Draconic Infraction within their own body. Wrapped around a ridgeline. Sacred across every dragon tradition as evidence that the most powerful thing a being with sufficient magical ability can do is use that ability to protect rather than dominate. The magics Aravoss sealed within themselves are **still there. Still sealed.**

---

## [[The Purified Garden]]

![[assets/landmark-purified-garden.jpg]]
**Location:** Green Dragon Province, deep forest interior  
**Type:** Sacred Site / Origin Point  
A concentration of planar corruption that the wood elves spent 412 years cleansing. Now an origin point — conditions for life so perfectly aligned that the process of evolution plays out in visible compressed form. Plants grow, change, seed, and develop new characteristics in the span of weeks rather than millennia. **Waiting list for endorsed visits: approximately four years.**

---

## [[The Grace of Death]]

![[assets/landmark-grace-of-death.jpg]]
**Location:** Undead Lands, Ordo Ossis territory  
**Type:** Sacred Site / Divine Marker  
A monolith carved in a script that predates every language currently spoken in the Isles — believed to be the Eioran tongue, which no one has been able to fully decode. Here, according to the most widely accepted theological account, the Eternal Patience granted the first mortals the gift of death. The Ceaseless Mother's presence here is, by careful arcane assessment, **real. Not active. Present.**

---

## [[The Ethereal Marsh]]

![[assets/landmark-ethereal-marsh.jpg]]
**Location:** Elven Isle, between Celesphain and Aelwrin Glade  
**Type:** Magical Phenomenon / Liminal Zone  
A strip of marshland existing in permanent between — the light always either dawn or dusk, shifting at irregular intervals. Said to cleanse the soul of those who pass through it honestly. The **reflections in the water do not always show what is above them**; those who pay attention report seeing scenes from other times. The Ebony Order monitors it as a matter of course.

---

## [[The Onyx Mountains]]

![[assets/landmark-onyx-mountains.jpg]]
**Location:** Border between the Undead Lands and the Black Dragon Province  
**Type:** Natural Barrier / Geological Formation  
A formation of black obsidian peaks formed during the Draconic Infraction — the residue of dragon-scale magical combat at a scale that literally changed the geology of the region. The single significant pass is maintained jointly by the Black Dragon Province and the Undead Lands' four ordos. The mountains' deep obsidian layers contain residues of the magical energies that formed them, and those residues are **slowly migrating toward the surface**. No one has produced a satisfactory explanation.

---

## [[The Eioran Vaults]]

![[assets/landmark-eioran-vaults.jpg]]
**Location:** Variable — Vaults appear and disappear across the Isles  
**Type:** Eioran Artifact / Active Mystery  
The workshops where the first race built the tools that shaped the world. They appear without warning — a structure of impossible geometry, standing in a location where nothing was standing the day before. They remain for between three and thirty days. Then they are gone.

No two Vault appearances have produced the same interior layout. The artefacts recovered from Vault interiors are among the most significant and most dangerous objects in the Ebony Order's deep archive. Since 2200 AF, Vault appearances have been **increasing in frequency**, and the pattern of their locations — previously random — has begun to show coherence. **The Order knows what it points toward. This information has not been shared publicly.**
