# Age of Empire — 9800 AB to 1 AF

#history #age-of-empire

> *Previous: [[Age of Saints]] · Next: [[Age of Ruin]]*

The final centuries before the Fracturing were defined by escalation. Everything that had been building across the previous ages — the elves' accumulated certainty, the younger races' growing power, the question of who got to decide what the world became — came to a head in an era of religious war, imperial conquest, and ultimately a catastrophe that redrew the map of the world entirely.

The Age of Empire ends with the Great Fracturing of **1 AF** — a weapon that failed and shattered the material plane.

---

## The Weeping Crusade — 9800 AB

A twenty-year religious war between followers of [[cosmology/The New Gods#Moran|Moran]] and [[cosmology/The New Gods#Kindra|Kindra]] over whether souls should be free to choose their afterlife — specifically, whether Kindra's cleansing flame was an obligation all souls must pass through or an option that should remain voluntary.

The theological disagreement was genuine. The violence it produced was also genuine. The Crusade demonstrated, with terrible clarity, **how thoroughly the same theology could be turned to violence by people who were entirely sincere**.

### The Role of Eloris
A weapons dealer later known as [[faith/Saints#Saint Eloris|Saint Eloris]] ran the supply chains on both sides of the Weeping Crusade — professionally, coldly, profitably. Their conversion and the role they played in disarming both sides in the Crusade's final years became one of the defining stories of what redemption could look like. They are now **venerated by veterans of every war since**.

---

## The Rise of Celeborn — c.9950 AB

The elven kingdom of Celeborn had been accumulating power for centuries. The argument they made to themselves was — as all arguments for empire eventually are — cast as service: the chaos of the fledgling races was a problem, and they could solve it.

Two elven houses dissented from the start:
- **House of Celzala** — tending the wounded left by the Crusade, treating those the army displaced
- **House of Varelis** — feeding communities driven from their agricultural lands

The main army moved anyway.

### The Arcane Engine — 9995 AB
Celeborn's forces completed an **arcane engine** designed to broadcast submission to all minds simultaneously — the final solution to the problem of other races' refusal to be organised. Its completion was announced as a triumph. It was operational for six years.

## The Great Fracturing — 1 AF

> *"The Great Fracturing of 1 AF was not a weapon deployed. It was a weapon that failed."*

When the arcane engine broke down under the pressure of the power it was trying to channel, it **broke the material plane instead**. The rifts it opened poured creatures from every elemental plane into the world simultaneously. The scale of what emerged was beyond anything the existing military or arcane infrastructure could contain.

### The Response
The dwarves, dragons, and giants **sacrificed enormous numbers of their populations** sealing those rifts. The effort took decades and left deep scars on all three peoples — the dwarves sealed their mountain gates and did not reopen them for centuries, the dragon population was severely reduced, the giant communities shrank to a fraction of their former size.

### The Judgement on the Elves
The elves were judged by the gods and found to have acted from **pride mistaken for benevolence** — which is the kind of error the gods take seriously. The curse laid on the elven people — that their bodies would be consumed by metal — was not simple punishment. It was reflection.

> *"The gold that spreads through elven flesh is the colour of the empire they built. Its spread is slow, and it can be slowed further by how one lives."*

The oldest elves, the ones who spent their longest years in genuine service to others, have learned to **shape the metal rather than be consumed by it**.

Two houses were treated differently:
- **House of Celzala** — silver-consuming curse shaped into something workable and beautiful; they became the dark elves, their transformation a mark of their choice
- **House of Varelis** — bronze metal that can be shaped by the bearer; the oldest wood elves are more metal than flesh, and use it as armour for the forests they tend

## Key Figures of the Age

### Saint Loujar — 9880 AB
The orc saint who spent decades demonstrating what orc civilisation actually was, countering centuries of misrepresentation through patient example. Their work reshaped how the older races perceived the orcs and earned a sainthood that, unusually, was asked for on behalf of a people rather than an individual.

→ Full profile: [[faith/Saints#Saint Loujar|Saint Loujar]]

### Saint Tothiri — 9900 AB
Still active during the Age of Empire, increasingly focused on locking away the dangerous knowledge that proliferating arcane experimentation was producing. The Ebony Order under Tothiri's leadership was in its most active classification period.

## The Central Lesson

The Age of Empire is the reason the modern world has the institutions it does — the Concord, the Order, the Senate, the Inquisition, the provincial system that prevents any one power from accumulating Celeborn-level dominance.

It is also the reason the world is so watchful about certainty. Celeborn's architects were not villains in their own understanding. They had identified a genuine problem — chaos, violence, waste, the suffering of disorder — and proposed a solution. They were entirely sure they were right.

> *"Nearly every catastrophe was caused by someone absolutely sure they were right."*
