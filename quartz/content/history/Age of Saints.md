# Age of Saints — 8300 to 9800 AB

#history #age-of-saints

> *Previous: [[Age of Law]] · Next: [[Age of Empire]]*

The Ebony Order and the Arcane Senate having been established, the world entered a period of relative institutional stability — which is to say: the large catastrophes were replaced by a succession of smaller ones, each managed by the frameworks that the previous catastrophes had prompted people to build. Into this period came the **first confirmed saints**, figures whose lives were so wholly given over to a principle that the gods themselves acknowledged them.

This is the age that defined what it means to live well in the Fractured Isles — not through legislation but through the lives of individuals whose examples have shaped every generation since.

---

## The First Saints

### Saint Korden — 7600 AB
The earliest confirmed saint, emerging from the turbulence following the [[Age of Law#The Ferrite Rebellion|Ferrite Rebellion]]. A dwarven heir who chose justice over inheritance and reshaped their civilisation's relationship with wealth. The first to demonstrate that sainthood was not for the pious — it was for those who acted.

→ Full profile: [[faith/Saints#Saint Korden|Saint Korden]]

### Saint Aestri — 7900 AB
Mapped the entirety of the Fractured Isles over the course of a single mortal lifetime and died content. For twenty years they flew routes no one had flown before, landing in territories that had never seen an aarakocra. The maps they returned with are still the foundation of the [[organisations/Pathfinders of Aestri|Pathfinders']] cartographic tradition.

→ Full profile: [[faith/Saints#Saint Aestri|Saint Aestri]]

### Saint Merrow — 8450 AB
Spent the [[Age of Law#The Crystal Plague|Crystal Plague]] years treating the sick across multiple provinces as their own body slowly crystallised. When the cure was finally achieved, [[cosmology/The New Gods#Vapos|Vapos]] descended to lift them to sainthood — the only recorded instance of a New God taking direct physical action in the material world.

→ Full profile: [[faith/Saints#Saint Merrow|Saint Merrow]]

## The Age of Prophecy — 9100 AB

The figure history calls the **Veiled Prophet** was a changeling who could see disaster coming before it arrived and who used that ability to prevent several catastrophic events. The question this raised — what to do with someone who can arrest people for crimes they have not yet committed — became too pointed to ignore.

The Veiled Prophet's disappearance and the subsequent dissolution of their cult prompted the [[organisations/The Chromatic Inquisition|Chromatic Inquisition]] to develop **the protocols around prophetic ability** that still govern its handling of seers today.

### Saint Ysolde — Disappeared 9200 AB
A painter whose work began producing accurate predictions. A scene of a flood, three months before the flood. A portrait of a soldier's knighting, eight months before the ceremony. A city burning, four years before the fire — with all architectural details precisely accurate.

After completing the final painting — five figures, an elemental cataclysm, titled *The End of All* — Ysolde **disappeared**. No body was ever found. The painting remains in the Brass Dragon Grand Library under the highest level of restricted access.

> *"Saint Ysolde's disappearance, a century after the Veiled Prophet, after completing a painting of five figures facing an elemental apocalypse, added a further layer of institutional anxiety around prophecy that has never quite resolved."*

→ Full profile: [[faith/Saints#Saint Ysolde|Saint Ysolde]]

## Further Saints of the Age

### Saint Zaivine — 9700 AB
A working bard and painter who travelled the Isles performing for anyone who needed it — from royal courts to field hospitals to rough taverns. Distinguished by attention rather than genius. Sainthood came from a lifetime of small deeds.

→ Full profile: [[faith/Saints#Saint Zaivine|Saint Zaivine]]

### Saint Varelis — 9650 AB
Led the wood elves out of the elven empire's crusade and spent their long life teaching farming to the communities the empire was driving from their land.

→ Full profile: [[faith/Saints#Saint Varelis|Saint Varelis]]

### Saint Farion — 9750 AB
United the feuding deep gnomes not through conquest but through diplomacy and the demonstration that creation and technology were better tools of connection than competition.

→ Full profile: [[faith/Saints#Saint Farion|Saint Farion]]

### Saint Tothiri — 9900 AB
Founded the Ebony Order. Gave the world systematic arcane education. Spent the second half of their life locking things away. The tension between these two acts remains the heart of the Order's identity.

→ Full profile: [[faith/Saints#Saint Tothiri|Saint Tothiri]]

## The Purification of the Garden — 9200 to 9600 AB

Four hundred and twelve years of sustained work by the wood elves of what is now the [[nations/Green Dragon Province|Green Dragon Province]], cleansing a concentration of planar corruption left over from the Fracturing. Completed in 9600 AB — the longest continuous project in recorded history, carried out across generations by a people who knew they would not live to see it finished.

The result is [[locations/Landmark Locations#The Purified Garden|The Purified Garden]] — now an origin point, conditions for new life so perfectly calibrated that evolution plays out in compressed, visible form.

## The Character of the Age

What distinguishes the Age of Saints is not its absence of catastrophe — the Crystal Plague alone killed hundreds of thousands — but its **pattern of response**. When the Crystal Plague came, someone spent their life treating it. When the elven empire began its expansion, someone led their people out rather than complicit. When the institutions that had been built began to calcify, someone challenged them.

The gods were paying attention. The saints are their acknowledgement.
