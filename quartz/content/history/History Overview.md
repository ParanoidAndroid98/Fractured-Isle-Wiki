# History of the Fractured Isles

#history

The history of the Fractured Isles is long enough that no single account can be fully trusted. What follows is the consensus of the Brass Dragon Grand Library, the Arcane Senate's historical faculty, and several centuries of Ebony Order records — the closest thing to an authoritative history the Isles possess.

**Dates:** AB = After Betrayal (from the Great Betrayal and imprisonment of the Outer Gods) · AF = After Fracturing (from the cataclysm that ended the Elven Empire). The two systems are not directly convertible.

---

## The Ages

### [[Age of Foundations]] — 0 to 1500 AB
The world nearly empty after the Great Betrayal. The proto-races — dragons, elves, and giants — emerge from the dissolved Eioran and begin repairing the plane. Younger races appear slowly.

### [[Age of Expansion]] — 1500 to 5200 AB
Populations grow. Ancient races build in earnest. Humans demonstrate remarkable capacity for social organisation and cultural transmission. The Wildheart Migration of the aarakocra occurs.

### [[Age of Law]] — 5200 to 8300 AB
The Draconic Infraction (200-year war over magic access). The Concord of Seven (6100 AB) formalises arcane law. The Ferrite Rebellion shapes dwarven culture. The Arcane Uprising creates the Ebony Order's detention function.

### [[Age of Saints]] — 8300 to 9800 AB
Relative institutional stability. First confirmed saints emerge. The Age of Prophecy (9100 AB) brings the Veiled Prophet and later Saint Ysolde.

### [[Age of Empire]] — 9800 AB to 1 AF
The Weeping Crusade (9800 AB). The fatal miscalculation of the elven empire of Celeborn. The Great Fracturing at 1 AF — the arcane engine fails, the material plane shatters.

### [[Age of Ruin]] — 1 to 300 AF
The world unrecognisable. Elder races spend themselves sealing rifts. The Undead Dominion founded (100 AF). Emperor Vauldris the Golden unites humanity (175 AF) using enslaved labour from other races.

### [[Age of Recovery]] — 300 to 1800 AF
Long, slow work of building a functional world. Dragon provinces consolidate. Dwarves seal their gates. The TideLock Agreement stabilises the shipping lanes. Worker protections emerge from the Relaxation Rebellion.

### [[The Modern Age]] — 1800 AF to Present (3000 AF)
The Pathfinders of Aestri map the known world. Provincial stability — but underneath it, Vault activity increasing, Marred cults resurgent, Greylands showing disturbances, Outer God prisons weakening.
