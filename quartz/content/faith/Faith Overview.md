# Faith & Power in the Fractured Isles

#faith #religion

Faith in the Fractured Isles operates in a world where the gods are **demonstrably real**. This is not a matter of theological debate. The Greylands is a place you can visit. Saints walk the world. Moran descended to lift Merrow to sainthood in direct physical form — the only documented case of a New God taking action in the material world, but enough to end the debate about whether the gods exist.

What faith grapples with is not whether the gods are real. It is **what they want**.

> *"No saint has ever been granted divinity while claiming exclusive truth."*

---

## The Structure of Faith

There is no unified church. Each of the [[cosmology/The New Gods|eight New Gods]] has their own traditions, clergy, and practice — and within each tradition, meaningful variation. The [[cosmology/The New Gods#Shangotha|Shangotha]] faiths range from gentle civic organisations to the institutional authority of the [[organisations/The Chromatic Inquisition|Chromatic Inquisition]]. The [[cosmology/The New Gods#Neithona|Neithona]] traditions range from dream journals kept by solitary wanderers to the intense prophetic community around [[faith/Saints#Saint Ysolde|Saint Ysolde's]] memory.

What they share:
- The gods communicate through the world, not through direct speech: weather, dreams, the granting of sainthood.
- No tradition claims exclusive truth, because the gods have made their position on exclusivity clear by who they have and haven't chosen to elevate.
- Clergy serve as **interpreters and facilitators**, not conduits for divine commands.

## Sainthood

See [[Saints]] for full individual profiles.

Sainthood is granted to those whose **lives became a demonstration of something** — a principle lived rather than preached. Upon death, all souls receive the choice between rebirth and rest. Saints receive a third option: to remain in the world in a transformed capacity.

**Key features of sainthood:**
- Granted posthumously (with rare exceptions Moran has not explained)
- Cannot be claimed, petitioned for, or earned through devotion alone
- Crosses racial, economic, and geographic lines — no single community has produced more saints than another
- Crosses faith lines — Eloris's sainthood was granted by Moran despite Eloris having no particular devotion to any god during their lifetime

## The Outer God Cults

The Outer Gods are imprisoned. Their cults still exist. The beings in question are accessible to those who know how — the planes that contain them are not impermeable, only structured such that ordinary people don't stumble through.

What the cults offer: direct contact with beings of genuine cosmic power who do not operate on the same framework as the New Gods. For people who find the New Gods' uncertainty and indirect communication frustrating, this is appealing. The Outer Gods, in their domains, are absolutely certain of everything and will tell you exactly what they think you should do.

The Inquisition's position: any contact with an imprisoned Outer God constitutes a destabilising influence and is subject to mandatory assessment. The Inquisition's capacity to enforce this position is, in practice, limited.

## The Marred "Faiths"

Not religions in any conventional sense. See [[cosmology/The Marred]] for full detail.

The people who join Marred-adjacent communities are not worshipping — they are being drawn by a resonance that feels, to the person being drawn, like recognition. The Marred do not ask for devotion. They leak corruption, and the corruption finds the people it fits.

The Chromatic Inquisition treats Marred cult involvement as a medical-adjacent situation as much as a religious one — the question is not "did this person choose wrong?" but "how far has the corruption progressed, and can it be reversed?"
